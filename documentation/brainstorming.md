## Sagrada influence and how it's reflected in **Vitreous Gloom**

To bridge the gap between the board game influence and **Vitreous Gloom's**  survival mechanics, the puzzle becomes the **"Stabilization Phase"** of your mission. In *Sagrada*, you draft dice; in *Vitreous Gloom*, you **extract the "dice" under fire.**

Here is how the puzzle mechanics integrate with the resource gathering and protection loops:

### 1. The "Extraction is Drafting" Mechanic
In a standard game of *Sagrada*, players take turns picking dice from a pool. In this game, the "pool" is the physical map.
* **Variable Purity (The Value):** When a Siphoner mines a node, the resulting Shard’s value ($1$–$6$) is determined by the **mining duration**.
    * *Short Extraction:* Yields low-value Shards ($1$–$2$) but generates little "Heat."
    * *Deep Extraction:* Yields high-value Shards ($5$–$6$) but creates massive noise, forcing you to defend the bot longer.
* **The Strategic Trade-off:** If your Window Pattern requires a "6 Cobalt," you are forced to commit to a high-noise extraction, directly linking the difficulty of the puzzle to the intensity of the horde defense.

### 2. Physical Inventory vs. The "Bin"
Unlike the board game, you don't have an infinite supply.
* **The Siphoner as a Courier:** The shards don't appear in your puzzle UI until the Siphoner physically returns to the base. 
* **Loss of Progress:** If a Siphoner is destroyed by zombies while carrying a "5 Ruby" you desperately needed for a corner slot, that "die" is lost. You must now find a new Ruby node or adapt your window pattern around the loss.

### 3. Aetheric Interference (Puzzle Constraints as Defense)
In *Sagrada*, placing a die incorrectly is a rule violation. In *Vitreous Gloom*, the constraints represent **Aetheric Stability**.
* **Feedback Loops:** If you place a shard that violates the adjacency rules (matching color or value), it doesn't just "not work"—it creates **Aetheric Noise**.
* **Mechanical Consequence:** An unstable window causes the "Lunar Veil" to flicker, making the zombies in the world faster or more aggressive. Correcting the puzzle effectively "quiets" the world, while struggling with the puzzle makes the protection phase harder.

### 4. Tool Cards vs. "Vitromancy" Skills
*Sagrada* has Tool Cards that let you move dice. Your game can turn these into **Resource-Consuming Abilities**:
* **The Lead Grinder:** Spend 10 raw Quartz to change a shard's value by $\pm 1$.
* **The Spectral Polish:** Spend a rare Ghost drop to ignore a color restriction for one cell.
* These tools ensure that even if you have "bad luck" with the shards you extracted, you can use your surplus resources to manipulate the puzzle.
